Overview

In Wings Of Fire: Darkness of Dragons, authored by Tui T. Sutherland, is a fantasy novel surrounding the adventures of Qibli, a SandWing hailed from the Sand Kingdom. He and his allies are to stop Darkstalker from conquering Pyrrhia before ot's too late. Please be warned that the Wings Of Fire series contains themes of war, violence, xenophobia, and death.

Overall Review

The prologue gives good insight on the main character's lineage and why his past was the way it is. It also establishes the world as ruthless to those who disobey and how almost everyone is only for themselves. The first chapter dives into this deeper when Qibli is first introduced as a dragonet in a post-war city of the Scorpion Den. The previous war of succession for the SandWing throne did a number on the population, especially their own civilians. Every dragon takes for themselves, and only for themselves, for there were not many resources to go around unless you're wealthy and your parents actually cared.

The following chapters features an older Qibli, now attending the Jade Mountain Academy, along with Winter, a former IceWing prince, assumed to be dead by his family and people from a previous book. Despite being a long-running series, the first few chapters still has some sort of exposition without completely spoiling any previous plot points beyond what's needed to get to know a character. The rest of the book follows Qibli as he sets out to find out what Darkstalker's up to, and trying to break Moonwatcher and some other dragons free from whatever spell Darkstalker casted on them. Qibli does get to grow as a character, and actually forages a bond with his friends rather than lamenting over what he isn't.

I will say that, in terms of a lesson, this series of the Wings Of Fire franchise in particular mostly focuses on "be yourself", to which the resolution of the whole Darkstalker conflict sort of brings into question as he gets enchanted to became a dragonet once again, removing all his powers, memories, and completely resetting his personality.
